The complete rules
Wealth: each site yields a harvest 1+σ(2u−1), u~U(0,1); the owner accumulates it. (σ=0: no fluctuations, wealth trajectories never cross, the trap never fires.)
Soldiers: S gains the income earned τ steps ago (so an untouched army is delayed wealth, S(t)=W(t−τ)); each war fought costs S→(1−c)S.
Each step, one random state (˜ = bloc sums):
 1. war on a rival neighbour that is richer (W̃e>W̃j: will out-arm you) but still weaker (S̃j>S̃e: today you win);
 2. else, if a rival bloc is stronger, propose an alliance to a random state — accepted iff the other party also faces a stronger rival (mutual benefit);
 3. else, with no threat left, walk away from its alliance.
War: allies decide one at a time — march iff your side, counting you, out-guns the troops committed opposite; declining has no other consequence. The winner takes one border province of the loser; a state on its last plot is instead vassalized: it keeps the plot, is torn from its alliance, and is bound into the victor's.
t = 0 circle radius ∝ √S̃ · lines = alliances (periodic boundaries)
