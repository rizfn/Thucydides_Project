Thucydides trap — minimal stochastic model

A two-parameter Gillespie process on a 2D lattice. Paper, code, applet, and empirical replication.

▶ Run the interactive applet 📄 Read the paper 📊 Empirical evidence note

What this is

Three reaction channels on a periodic lattice — growth, war, fragmentation — produce the qualitative phenomenology of the Thucydides trap: mutual growth raises the per-pair war rate, an Ibn Khaldūn sustainability ceiling makes large states brittle, the resulting dynamics give heavy-tailed war-size distributions, sharp multipolar–hegemonic transitions, and recurrent rise-and-fall cycles of dominant powers.
